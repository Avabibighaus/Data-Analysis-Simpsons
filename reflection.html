SIMPSONS ANALYSIS

By Aarohi Rawat, Ava Bibighaus, Shreya Tangirala

Introduction

The Simpsons is an iconic and influential animated sitcom. It is known for its satirical depiction of American life portrayed through the everyday experiences of the Simpson Family: Homer, Marge, Bart, Lisa, and Maggie. This project focuses on season 21 of The Simpsons, examining how each family member contributed to the overall success of the show. By studying the twenty first season of The Simpsons, we can see how character presence has contributed to audience engagement.

To explore this, we created three graphs: a line graph showing the views for each episode across the season in the U.S., a line graph comparing the number of lines each family member has spoken in each episode, and a scatterplot of IMDb ratings received per episode in the twenty first season. Using these graphs, we can answer our question: Which Simpson family member had the strongest presence in Season 21, and how does their presence relate to the episode ratings and popularity?
