DATA/ANALYSIS

This graph shows the number of views (in millions) for each episode in season 21. As we can see episode 10 holds the absolute max views for the graph shooting up to about 15. After that peak it sharply drops back down by episode 11. From episodes 11-23 the number of views stays fairly stable between 5 and 6.5 million views. There are minor fluctuations but no major increase or decrease after the initial spike. This analysis shows an overall trend of an early surge in popularity, then viewership levels out and remains consistent for the rest of the season showing a loyal viewer base.


This graph displays the number of spoken lines for each member of the Simpson family across every episode in Season 21. Each character is represented by a different color, allowing us to easily compare how much dialogue each character receives throughout the season. From the trends, we can see that Homer consistently has the most lines, reinforcing his role as the primary focus of the show. Maggie has the fewest lines, which is expected since she rarely speaks. Bart and Lisa often have similar line counts, although their involvement varies depending on the episode’s storyline. Overall, this graph highlights how dialogue is distributed within the family, emphasizing Homer’s central role and the shifting focus between Bart, Lisa, and Marge from episode to episode.


This graph shows IMDb episode ratings for season 21. IMDb ratings are rated out of 10. From the graph we can see that the ratings for most episodes fall between 6.5 and 7.2, which indicates generally positive reviews. The ratings don't appear to change dramatically from episode to episode. Through the data we can see a minimum around the middle of the season but discarding that point the data stays at a median rating of 6.85. This stipulates that episode ratings stay consistent through the season and the Simpsons series has an overall high rating.
